# BMW GLOBAL SALES DASHBOARD (2018 to 2025)

## PROJECT OVERVIEW

This project analyzes BMW vehicle sales data from 2018 to 2025 using an interactive Excel dashboard. The objective is to transform raw sales data into clear, data driven business insights that help understand model performance, regional demand, revenue drivers, and the growth of electric vehicles.

The analysis quantifies key performance drivers by measuring revenue contribution across models, evaluating geographic distribution of sales, and assessing relationships between external variables such as fuel prices and vehicle demand. The dashboard enables stakeholders to quickly identify high impact models, track EV growth, and monitor overall sales performance across regions and time.


## BUSINESS PROBLEM

BMW sells vehicles across multiple regions, models, and price segments. Without structured analysis, it becomes difficult to quickly identify which models drive revenue, how demand changes over time, and which markets perform the best.

This project answers several key business questions:

- Which BMW models generate the highest revenue?
- How are sales distributed across regions?
- What are the monthly and yearly sales patterns?
- How quickly are electric vehicles growing?
- Is there any relationship between fuel prices and vehicle sales?

The dashboard consolidates these insights into a single view to make performance monitoring easier.

## KEY INSIGHTS

Analysis of the dashboard reveals the following key patterns:

- The top 3 models X7, iX, and X5 contribute approximately 46.7 percent of total revenue, indicating strong revenue concentration in a small set of high performing models.  

- Expanding to the top 4 models including i4, these contribute nearly 60 percent of total revenue, reinforcing the importance of premium and high value vehicle segments.  

- Electric vehicle models iX and i4 together contribute approximately 28 percent of total revenue, highlighting the growing importance of EVs in BMW’s portfolio.  

- Revenue distribution across regions is highly balanced, with China contributing approximately 25.5 percent of total units sold, while Europe, USA, and Rest of World each contribute between 24.7 percent and 24.9 percent. The difference between the highest and lowest regions is less than 1 percent, indicating a well diversified global market presence.  

- There is a significant performance gap across models, with the highest revenue model X7 generating over 2.3 times the revenue of lower performing models such as MINI.  

- Fuel price and vehicle sales show a weak positive correlation of 0.20, indicating a minimal relationship between fuel price changes and overall vehicle demand. This suggests that fuel prices are not a strong independent driver of BMW sales within the observed period.  

- The observed relationship is correlational and does not imply causation, as other factors such as product mix, regional demand, and macroeconomic conditions likely play a larger role in influencing sales.  


## BUSINESS RECOMMENDATIONS

Based on the analysis, the following actions are recommended:

- Prioritize high revenue models such as X7, iX, and X5, which together contribute approximately 46.7 percent of total revenue. Increasing production allocation and targeted marketing for these models can significantly improve overall profitability.  

- Accelerate electric vehicle expansion, as BEV share has grown from approximately 2 percent in 2018 to nearly 19 percent in 2025. Expanding EV availability and infrastructure support in key markets can help capture this rapidly increasing demand.  

- Focus on top performing regions such as China, USA, and Rest of World, each contributing over 6 million units sold during the analysis period. These regions should be prioritized for marketing investments and supply chain optimization.  

- Optimize inventory and production planning based on seasonal demand patterns observed in monthly sales trends. Aligning supply with peak demand periods can improve inventory turnover and reduce holding costs.  

- Incorporate fuel price sensitivity into pricing and promotional strategies. As fuel price fluctuations show alignment with changes in sales patterns, introducing financing offers or incentives during high fuel price periods can help sustain demand.  


## LIMITATIONS AND ASSUMPTIONS

- The dataset is assumed to be complete and accurate for the given time period.  
- External factors such as competitor actions, policy changes, and supply chain constraints are not included in the analysis.  
- The relationship between fuel prices and sales is observational and does not imply causation.  
- Some variables such as GDP growth are considered at a high level and may not fully capture regional economic differences.
